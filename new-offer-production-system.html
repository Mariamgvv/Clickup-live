1 Why This Exists

We are building an offer engine — not just shipping funnels. The goal is to create offers that can be researched, built, launched, and either scaled or killed based on real data, on a repeatable cadence.

This SOP covers the full lifecycle: from deciding what offer to build, through research, content production, funnel build, QA, launch, and the post-launch decision point. Every phase has an owner, a definition of done, and a gate that must be passed before the next phase starts.

Days 7–10

Technical execution only. Everything the GHL team needs — copy, creatives, funnel architecture, approved emails — must be ready before this phase starts. This phase follows Ivona's existing funnel SOPs exactly.

Output: Full funnel built · Automations connected · Ads ready · Tracking live
Phase 5
QA — End-to-End Testing
Days 10–13 (3–4 days)
Owner: Irfan signs off · Mariam coordinates

This is the gate between Stage 2 (Install) and Stage 3 (Launch) in Matt's model. QA is 3–4 days — not an afternoon. Rushing QA is how tracking breaks, attribution is lost, and emails fire wrong. Matt was explicit: push QA if needed, never push the launch without it.

  • Full funnel walkthrough — mobile and desktop, incognito from ad URL to thank-you page
  • Form submission — contact created correctly in CRM, all fields populated
  • UTM capture — source, medium, campaign, content, ad ID all in contact record
  • Pixel and CAPI events — verified in Meta Events Manager, deduplication checked
  • All emails received — welcome, 24h, 4h, 15min — correct links, correct timing
  • SMS received — if applicable
  • Calendar links working — Google Calendar adds correctly, ICS downloads and imports
  • Booking flow — if applicable, booking confirmation fires, reminder sequence starts
  • CRM tags applied correctly — workflow triggers as expected, no duplicate sequences
  • Ad URLs verified — each ad URL tested manually with full UTM string
Gate: Irfan signs off on QA. No launch without his sign-off. Not Mariam, not Matt — Irfan.
Output: QA checklist completed and filed in ClickUp · All issues resolved · Launch cleared
Phase 6
Launch & First 48 Hours
Day 13–14
Owner: Irfan · Mariam assists

Stage 3 in Matt's model. Ads go live and we run a controlled 3–5 day validation window to collect the first real performance data before making any optimization decisions.

  • Ads activated — campaigns turned on with spend cap confirmed
  • Traffic flow checked — first clicks reaching landing page correctly
  • First registrations / leads confirmed — CRM receiving contacts, attribution correct
  • Pixel events firing in real traffic — not just test events
  • Baseline metrics captured — CPL, CTR, CVR within first 48h
  • Offer added to Monday Funnel Report for weekly tracking
  • Any launch-day bugs flagged immediately to Irfan — do not wait
Offer is LIVE when all of these are true

✓Tracking confirmed live — pixel, CAPI, and UTMs all working with real traffic

Phase 7
Analyze → Scale or Kill
Week 2–4 post-launch
Owner: Mariam pulls data · Matt makes the call

This is Stage 4 of Matt's model — Validation & Optimization. Every offer gets a decision point after the controlled 3–5 day validation window. Matt's model: run the webinar, analyze, then scale the winners, refine the angle, or test a new offer entirely.

  • Metrics reviewed after first webinar/launch window
  • CPL (cost per lead), CTR, landing page CVR
  • Show-up rate (target: 30%+ for webinar)
  • Booking rate (% of attendees who book a call)
  • Close rate (% of booked calls that convert)
  • ROAS or revenue generated
  • Qualitative signals checked
  • Lead quality (Irfan / sales team feedback)
  • Drop-off points in funnel (Clarity heatmaps)
  • No-show reasons if available
  • Decision made in Sprint Review — documented in ClickUp, not left in Slack

Matt's exact language from the Funnel Install Journey: "Data determines the next move — Scale / Refine angle / Test new offer."

Scale
  • CPL within target range
  • Show-up rate above 30%
  • Positive booking or purchase rate
  • ROAS positive or near breakeven
  • Action: increase ad spend, run more webinars, optimize creatives — same offer, more volume
Refine Angle
  • Core offer is right, messaging or targeting is off
  • 1–2 metrics close to target but not there
  • Clear hypothesis for what to change
  • Action: 1 iteration sprint — new hook, new creative angle, or adjusted audience. Retest once. If still misses → Kill
Test New Offer
  • Core metrics missed across the board
  • Refine angle attempted with no improvement
  • Offer-market fit is genuinely wrong
  • Action: shut down ads, archive funnel, log learnings in ClickUp — move to next offer in pipeline
Output: Decision documented in ClickUp · Learnings filed · Next offer in production or scale plan in place
Phase 8
Creation + Content Creation
Parallel to Phase 3 or post-Phase 7
Owner: Ivona/Mircea team · Irfan included

This phase covers the actual production of offer assets — not copy or funnel pages, but the real content that gets delivered to the customer. Ivona and Mircea lead this. Irfan is included for technical review and alignment on what gets produced.

  • Offer asset creation — actual deliverables the customer receives
  • Video content, modules, sessions, recordings
  • Course curriculum built out in full — not just outlined
  • PDFs, frameworks, tools, downloads included in the offer
  • Webinar presentation (slides + speaker notes) — final version, not draft
  • Content aligned to the offer brief — transformation promise must be deliverable
  • Review Phase 2 brief before starting — creation must match what was promised
  • Any mismatch between brief and what can be created → flag to Mariam before building
  • Mircea — visual and video production
  • Ad video creatives where applicable
  • Presentation design and visual polish
  • Branded asset templates
  • Ivona — content strategy and production lead
  • Oversees full content creation process
  • Ensures content quality matches offer promise
  • Signs off before handoff to GHL team
  • Irfan — technical and strategic review
  • Reviews content for accuracy and feasibility
  • Confirms delivery mechanism aligns with what GHL/platform can support
Gate: Ivona signs off on all created assets. Irfan confirms delivery mechanism is technically sound. Handoff to Phase 4 (Funnel Build) only when complete.
Output: All offer content created and approved · Assets ready for funnel integration · No empty promises in the offer

6 Non-Negotiables

Research before build — always
No funnel gets built without Phase 1 research done and the Go/No-Go confirmed. No exceptions. Irfan's exact note: "totally minimal without this."
Actual content before funnel
The offer content — slides, script, curriculum — must exist before GHL build starts. We are building real products, not empty funnels.
Matt approves all emails
Email sequences reviewed and approved by Matt before they go into GHL. Not after. Not during QA. Before.
Irfan signs off QA
Nothing launches without Irfan's QA sign-off. Not Mariam, not the GHL team — Irfan. He owns the technical gate.
No scope mid-build
Phases 1–3 consolidate all requirements. Once Phase 4 starts, scope is locked. New ideas go into the backlog for the next offer.
Every offer gets a decision
Phase 7 is not optional. Every offer gets a Scale / Iterate / Kill decision, documented in ClickUp. No offer drifts without a call.
Blockers flagged by Day 7
Mariam flags any production risk by Day 7. Never on the day before QA. Matt gets one clear message: new date + root cause.
QA is 3–4 days, not 1
Rushing QA is how tracking breaks and attribution is lost. If production runs long, push the launch date — not the QA window.

7 If an Offer Misses the Deadline

Miss Protocol
  • Mariam flags the risk by Day 7 — never on the day before QA or launch
  • Matt receives one clear message: what slipped, why, and the new confirmed launch date
  • QA window is never compressed — push the launch, not the QA
  • The miss does not cancel the next offer — both run on their own timelines
  • Root cause documented in ClickUp and reviewed in the next Sprint Retrospective
  • If the same phase misses twice, the SOP gets updated — not the team blamed
