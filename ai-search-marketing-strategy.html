AI Search Marketing — Strategy Analysis

AEO / GEO tactics for AI Sponsor & Guru products
Prepared for Matt & product teams · June 18, 2026
Captures the AI-search marketing tactics we've been collecting — what each is, how it works, cost, risk, and where it fits. AI Sponsor first, then replicate across DRM & Rekindle.

The bet — why this matters

Search is shifting from Google blue links → AI answers (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Being the answer an AI cites is becoming as important as ranking on Google — and it's a different game:

  • Neil Patel's data: Rank #1 on Google ≈ 31% chance of an AI citation; rank #4 ≈ 2.6%. And 75% of AI citations go to pages that don't rank in Google's top 10. → Google rank and AI citation are two separate games.
  • Why it matters for us: free / low-cost traffic vs. paying blog writers or Meta ads. Company-wide initiative — build the playbook on AI Sponsor, then replicate.

Two new disciplines beyond classic SEO: AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — both mean "be the answer AI gives."

The spectrum: Whitehat → Grayhat → Blackhat

TypeDefinitionDetection / legal risk
WhitehatEarn visibility by genuinely being the clearest/best answerNone — durable
GrayhatPay to amplify real things, or use loopholes (not strictly fake)Moderate — can be reclassified as black over time
BlackhatFake / buy signals to trick the AI (fake reviews, bought comments)High — bans, signal decay, some illegal

We're documenting the full spectrum. Each tactic below is labelled with its hat-color and its real downside so the call is informed.

The tactics

A. On-site foundation Whitehat ✓ already built for AI Sponsor

What: Structured data (JSON-LD), an FAQ in real Q&A form, llms.txt, and pages with clear structure, direct answers and fresh data (the exact traits Neil Patel says cited pages share).

How it helps: makes the page machine-readable so AI engines can extract & cite it.

Cost: low (our build time).   Risk: none.

AI Sponsor: DONE (live on getaisponsor.com).   Other products: template — replicate on DRM / Rekindle.

B. Citation tracking / measurement Whitehat

What: Tools that query Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini and show which of your (and competitors') pages get pulled into AI answers, so you can reverse-engineer the winners.

Tool options (same job): vibha-ramprakash "SEO + GEO Autopilot" tracker · Ubersuggest (Neil Patel) · DataForSEO (cheap API).

Cost: small subscription / API — needs go-ahead.   Risk: none.

Fit: all products — this is the "are we winning" dashboard.

Tool deep-dive — the "SEO + GEO Autopilot" tracker Whitehat

The tracker Matt found (vibha-ramprakash.github.io/seo-geo-tracker) is a worked example of the Category B job — a static dashboard that runs a continuous loop: Audit → Diagnose → Strategize → Fix → Re-audit. Four modules:

ModuleWhat it does
The AuditorTechnical crawl + live Google ranking data (Search Console) + impressions/CTR + Core Web Vitals + an AI-search readiness score (0–100); surfaces the top 5 impact problems.
The AI-Search SpyTests whether AI engines actually cite your site; scores AI-search visibility (0–100) across 5 major AI platforms; flags reasons for non-inclusion.
The DiagnoserFinds striking-distance keywords (Google positions 8–20), maps the top questions asked in AI search by intent, highlights content gaps.
The FixerGenerates deployment-ready output: FAQ schema (JSON-LD), llms.txt, robots.txt, rewritten titles/meta/answer blocks. ← this is the part we already did by hand for AI Sponsor

Data sources: Google Search Console, DataForSEO (~<$1/1,000 lookups), free AI-visibility checkers (Ahrefs / Semrush).

Can we build it? Yes — it's a static dashboard (our wheelhouse). Phased: (1) on-site Fixer outputs ✓ done; (2) dashboard shell + Search Console/PageSpeed (free); (3) paid keyword data + AI-citation checking (needs budget).

Reality check: getaisponsor.com is ~1 week old, so the tracking modules show little data for the first few weeks — value compounds over time. The on-site Fixer pays off immediately.

C. Content & authority White → Gray

What: Publish useful content where AI engines harvest it — own blog, Medium, Substack, plus earning real press.

Whitehat: genuinely useful articles answering the questions people ask AI.

Grayhat — "parasite hosting": publish (often AI-generated) content on high-authority platforms (Medium/Substack) with links back, to borrow their domain authority.

Cost: low–medium.   Risk: low for genuine content; parasite hosting can be de-ranked if platforms crack down.

Fit: strong for AI Sponsor (genuine recovery content) and all products.

D. Reddit & community signals White → Black

What: LLMs treat Reddit as a "source of truth" and pull from it heavily — presence there directly influences AI answers.

Whitehat: genuinely participate in relevant subreddits, earn authentic mentions.

Blackhat: buy fake comments/upvotes on Reddit (and Facebook/YouTube) to fake popularity to LLMs.

Cost: low (fake) / time (genuine).   Risk (blackhat): high — Reddit aggressively detects & bans astroturfing; recovery subreddits especially.

AI Sponsor: Reddit = high-leverage whitehat; blackhat here is the highest-risk option given the recovery audience (fakery becomes the story). Other products: Reddit valuable; blackhat ban-prone everywhere.

E. Press placement services Gray → Black

What: Paid services placing brand articles on Entrepreneur, Forbes, Yahoo (lower tier) or Reuters, Fortune (~$750–$1,000). AI trusts high-authority publications, so placement boosts AI recommendations.

Cost: ~$750–$1,000+ per high-tier placement.   Risk: moderate — pay-to-play, not fully fake; widely used; lower detection risk than fake reviews.

AI Sponsor: viable — plus a genuine story to pitch (the 85-person WhatsApp group, the one-for-one model) that could earn real coverage too. Other products: viable across the board.

F. Fake reviews Blackhat — & illegal

What: Buying fake reviews/testimonials to signal quality to users and AI.

Cost: low.   Risk: highest.

Legal note: The US FTC rule banning fake / bought reviews & testimonials took effect in 2024, with civil penalties per violation — plus platform removal and reputational damage.

AI Sponsor: recommend keeping this off AI Sponsor specifically — fabricated testimonials on a recovery product is legal exposure + a trust landmine with a vulnerable audience. Real beta testimonials (consent + anonymity) get the same benefit safely. Other products: FTC-exposed everywhere; lowest-recommendation tactic.

Risk reality (for the decision)

  • Fake reviews = legal exposure (FTC, 2024). Fact, not caution.
  • Bought signals decay — AI companies actively fight manipulation; tactics that work today can be clawed back, taking the brand's standing with them.
  • Platform bans — Reddit especially.
  • Brand risk scales with audience sensitivity — highest for AI Sponsor (recovery), lower for less-sensitive products.
  • Grayhat (press placement, parasite hosting) is the pragmatic middle — widely used, lower detection/legal risk, real upside.
